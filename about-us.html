Why know about flags?

Flags are important because they symbolise a country and provide a message to the rest of the world about how they would like to be perceived. A good example of this is the flag of the United States of America. It has 50 stars to symbolise the unity of the 50 current states; stars were added every time a new state joined the union. The stripes represent the original 13 states within the union.

The significance of capitals

Capital cities are usually the base of judicial and economic functions within a country and primarily have the highest populations and centers of activity in general. They are the head of a country and contain the political leaders who control the area.
